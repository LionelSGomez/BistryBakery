Inverted banana cake

Ingredients

  • 2 large or 3 small bananas
  • 2 eggs
  • cups of flour 0000
  • half a cup of neutral oil
  • half a cup of muscabo sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • Vanilla essence or zest of some citrus
  • Walnuts (optional)
  • Chocolate chunks (optional)
  • For the caramel: 1 cup of sugar, 2 tablespoons of water
  • For the base: banana (the amount necessary to cover the base of the pan) and 2 tablespoons of butter

How to make the inverted banana cake

In a saucepan, make the caramel with the sugar and the two tablespoons of water. It should be a light caramel. Spread the caramel on an oven-safe mold covering the entire base and edges. Once it is caramelized, butter it, as if it were a normal mold. Covering everything. Cut bananas into one centimeter slices and distribute around the base. Reserve on one side. Put the chopped bananas and eggs in the blender. Blend at maximum power until pureed. Incorporate (always in the blender) the oil, the vanilla essence or the zest. Blend until incorporated. Add the sugar and the sifted flour together with the baking powder little by little. If the blender is too small, it can be done in parts or directly, once you have the banana and egg puree, incorporate the rest in a bowl. Once everything is well incorporated, add the large chopped nuts and chocolate. Mix well. Pour over the mold with caramel. Place in a preheated 180 Â° oven for about an hour. Or until when puncturing the preparation, the dry toothpick comes out. Unmold as soon as it comes out of the oven. If it does get a little stuck, take the mold to the stove to melt the caramel and try again. Serve the banana cake alone or with a scoop of ice cream. To enjoy!!
